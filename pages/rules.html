Programs & Functions

Terms
  • Functions - A type of card. Equipped to units or used individually.
  • Program - A type of card. A unit used in play.

  • Process - Action taken by a Program. Usually the resolution of a Function.
  • Execution - Player called program use. Executions take place after the process they are activated during finishes.
  • Activated - Program state. Face-up. Programs only start processes while active.
  • Deactivated - Program state. Face-down. Programs can maintain nulls, but cannot start processes.

  • Compiler Processing Units(CPU) - These are required to play cards.
  • Reputation(REP) - The Health of each player.
  • Integrity(INT) - The Health of each program.
  • Resistance(RES) - Reduce all incoming damage by this amount.
  • Charge(CHA) - Increase all outgoing damage by this amount.
  • Speed(SPD) - Programs with higher speed are called first.
  • Immune(IMMUNE) - Cannot take damage or be destroyed.

  • Null - When Function does not fit the requirements of play. Null functions are destroyed when revealed. Having a null revealed lowers reputation by 1.
  • Uncalled(Bluff) - When a Function is equipped to a Program without a slot, or without the proper Properties.
  • Unfinished(Bluff) - When a Function is played face down without spending the required CPU.

  • Height - The position of a program. New programs are above previous programs. On the board, left is lowest, and right is the highest.
  • Base Program(Bottom) - A player's lowest program.
  • Surface Program(Top) - A player's highest program.
Goals
  • Reduce the opposing player's Reputation to 0 at the end of a Cycle.
  • Have the most Reputation after 10(Standard) Cycles.
How to Play
  • Player Decks of 24. Limit of 3 copies of a card per deck.
  • Starting Reputation of 10.
  • Both Draw a starting hand of 7.
  • Flip a coin? to decide Player Order and starting player.
  • Begin looping through cycles(turns).

  • Cycle Starts.
  • All players draw 1 card.
  • Set player CPU(Compiler Processing Units) equal to the cycle number, up to a limit of 6.
  • Loop through players' Main Phases, in Player Order.
  • Loop through players' Activation Phases, in Player Order.
  • Loop through players' Targeting Phases, in Player Order.
  • Begin Execution Phases.
  • Players without programs lose REP equal to the cycle number.
  • Start next cycle with a new Player Order. The first player last turn is moved to the last player this turn, and all other palyers move up in priority.

  • Cycle limit is can be variable but standard is 10 for 2 players, and 16 for 4 players.
  • A player wins by having the most reputation by the end of all cycles, or by reducing all opposing player's Reputation to 0. When a player has a Reputation of 0, they no longer participate in the game.
Main Phase
  • Players spend CPU to play Programs and Equip Functions.
  • Programs can be played face up or face down. If a Program is face down, all of it's functions must also be face down. When a program is played, it is positioned above (to the right of) a player's other programs.
  • Support Functions must always face the same way as their programs.
  • A program can be overloaded with functions, having more functions than slots. In this case, when revealed, the null functions are destroyed and the player takes reputation damage.
  • CPU can be added to face down cards, allowing players to Finish an Unfinished program.
Activation Phase
  • Players can choose to flip their Programs face up, Activating them.
  • Players can choose to flip their Programs face down, Deactivating them.
Targeting Phase
  • Only occurs when there are more than 2 players.
  • Players choose who their target player will be for the Execution Phase.
Execution Phase
  • Constructors resolve based on Order of Operations.
  • Then, Calls resolve based on Order of Operations.
Order of Operations
  • Cards do not take effect unless Active (face up).
  • Each player, in Player Order, take alternating turns resolving Functions.
  • For each player, the lowest active Program is selected, and its Functions are resolved in order of lowest to highest. After a Program is complete, the next lowest Program is selected.

Example: Players Red and Blue. Red goes first with programs A (2 Functions) and B (2 Functions), and Blue goes second with programs C (1 Function) and D (3 Functions). Order of Function resolution would be as follows.
  • Red_A_1
  • Blue_C_1
  • Red_A_2
  • Blue_D_1
  • Red_B_1
  • Blue_D_2
  • Red_B_2
  • Blue_D_3

  • In the case of a Program being activated during the Execution Phase, the player's current Program does not change until the previous Program is completed.
  • Higher Speed Programs activate earlier. Order of Operations applies to two Programs of the same Speed.
  • Functions are flipped face up when activated. All Functions of Active Programs must be activated and resolved.
  • Executions can be used during this time. When a player uses an Execution from their hand, the effect takes place after the current Program's current Process.
